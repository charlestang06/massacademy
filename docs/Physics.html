Physics

Our physics class is taught by Mrs. Chase. This class follows the AP Physics C curriculum and we perform a variety of labs throughout the year. In this class, we learn topics ranging from kinematics to electricity and magnetism. In A term, we covered kinematics and dynamics. In B term, we learned about momentum, collisions, and rotational motion. The problems we tackle in this class are generally more difficult than traditional textbooks, and we learn problem solving skills throughout the course.

Acceleration Investigation

This motion investigation was the first lab of A term, where we used motion carts and low friction inclined planes to determine if a cart had constant acceleration or constant speed. Then, we wrote our results from the lab in a Claim Evidence Reasoning (CER) format.

Dynamics Lab

In this lab, we were tasked with creating our own investigation to determine if Newton's Laws are consistent with observations. David Barsoum, Peter Liang, and I designed an experiment where we analyzed the frictional forces with varying weights. See the attached document below for our final report.
