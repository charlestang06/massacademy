STEM II

STEM II is taught by Dr. Crowthers in the second half of the year, where we develop assistive devices in groups. In STEM II, we follow the engineering design process in groups, where we design, develop, and test a device that can support someone with a disability.

SEARCH: Smart Electronic Assistance and Retrieval Companion for Home

For the STEM II project, I worked with Joseph Yu, Tarun Eswar, and Nevin Thinagar. Throughout this project, I was the Chief-Informational-Officer (CIO) to lead documentation processes.

Problem Description

Over 40% of people will experience some form of memory loss by the time they turn 60. While memory loss and mental impairments come in many forms, one of the most common symptoms is misplacing essential objects such as wallets, keys, hearing aids, and glasses. This significantly impacts the ability of older adults to live independently. Coupled with declining eyesight and physical strength, searching through an entire living space for objects can be highly strenuous for older adults and can often be unsuccessful. While current widespread solutions in production to track and locate missing objects, such as Air Tags and Tile Trackers, use Bluetooth transmitters, the resolution of these devices is limited to areas often more extensive than the entire house, making it unsuitable for isolating a specific area of the house for an older adult to search.

Approach

Our design will be a rover-based device equipped with computer vision technologies that can be a powerful tool for locating misplaced items. The device can navigate autonomously through an environment on the ground while using its onboard cameras and advanced object recognition algorithms to identify and locate specific objects. Once an item has been located, the device can alert the user to its location through a cloud-based mobile application. This technology has the potential to save time and reduce frustration by quickly locating lost or misplaced items.
