Humanities

Instead of the traditional history and English classes taken at my sending school, Mass Academy offers a humanities course taught by Ms. Small that combines aspects of traditional English classes, history, sociology, psychology, and anthropology. Over the course of the year, we discuss the question "What does it mean to be human?" through a variety of perspectives. For A term, we discussed Walden, by Henry David Thoreau, and education in America. In B term, we learned about satires and wrote our own satirical analysis.

Education Synthesis Essay

In A term, we discussed the impacting factors of education and how we can improve it. As a final assignment for this topic, we wrote an essay about education. I chose to write about inclusivelness, mental health, and communities within an educational community.

Satirical Analysis Essay

In B term, we discussed the key elements of satires and analyzed satires for their effectiveness. For the final assignment of this module, I compare and contrasted two satires on their tone and delivery. The first satire, A Modest Proposal, by Jonathan Swift, satirizes eating babies to solve the problem of poverty in the 1700s. The second satire, Babycakes, by Neil Gaiman, satirizes eating babies to highlight the issue of waste and environmental damage.
